A gold coin is a coin that is made mostly or entirely of gold. Most gold coins minted since 1800 are 90–92% gold (22 karat), while most of today's gold bullion coins are pure gold, such as the Krugerrand, Canadian Maple Leaf, and Australian Kangaroo.
Alloyed gold coins, like the American Gold Eagle and South African Krugerrand, are typically 91.7% gold by weight, with the remainder being silver and copper. Traditionally (up to about the 1930s), gold coins have been circulation coins, including coin-like bracteates and dinars. In recent decades, however, gold coins are mainly produced as bullion coins for investors and as commemorative coins for collectors.
While modern gold coins are still legal tender, they are not observed in everyday financial transactions, as the metal value normally exceeds the nominal value. For example, the American Gold Eagle has a denomination of 10 USD, but a metal value of more than $800 USD (being around a half an ounce). The gold reserves of central banks are dominated by gold bars, but gold coins may occasionally contribute.

Gold has been used as money for many reasons. It is fungible, with a low spread between the prices to buy and sell.
Gold is also easily transportable, as it has a high value-to-weight ratio compared to other commodities such as silver. Gold can be re-coined, divided into smaller units, or melted into larger units such as gold bars, without destroying its metal value. The density of gold is higher than most other metals, making it difficult to pass counterfeits. Additionally, gold is extremely unreactive chemically: it does not tarnish or corrode over time.

Short history of gold

The history of gold is long connected with money, but gold relinquished this role in developed economies after the outbreak of the Second World War. At the end of the war, the Bretton Woods monetary system, a regime of fixed exchange rates, was created. This system broke down in 1971 when the US unilaterally ended its gold standard, which set the convertibility of gold and the dollar to US$35 per ounce. References to the Gold Standard often refer to two key periods in history: that of the Classical Gold Standard and that of the post Bretton Woods gold-pegged exchange rate system.

Most popular golden coins

Krugerrand

When you think of South African gold, it is likely you that the world famous Krugerrand and Rand coins will spring to mind. Many people buy Krugerrand coins as an investment in physical gold. This gold coin has a long history and was once the world's best-selling investment currency and became especially popular in America. The success of this coin has also encouraged other countries to mint their very own gold coins, such as the Maple Leaf, which was first issued in Canada in 1979. First Krugerrand was minted in 1967. It was the first coin to be minted in exactly 1 troy ounce of gold, which is now the standard weight for gold trading.
Today, this coin is still minted by the Rand Refinery in South Africa and the Krugerrand remains one of the best known investment coins. The obverse of this gold coin depicts Paul Kruger, the fifth president of South Africa. On the reverse of the coin is a springbok, an animal that symbolizes the African wilderness. Due to an alloy of gold and copper, this coin has a recognizable red-gold color. In addition, the Krugerrand has a purity of 22 carats (916.7 / 1000).

Vienna Philharmonic

The 1 Troy ounce gold Vienna Philharmonic coin is issued by the Austrian Mint and minted in the highest purity of 24 carat (99.99% pure) gold. Between 1989 and 2001, the coin's nominal value was expressed in schillings. In 2001, after the introduction of the Euro this was changed accordingly, making it the first gold coin to be minted in the Euro currency. The Vienna Philharmonic coin has remained one of the world's most popular investment coins since its introduction in 1989. Vienna's world famous philharmonic orchestra was the inspiration for the coin's design and has remained the same since its issue.

Marple Leaf

The 1 Oz Maple Leaf 2022 gold coin series remains immensely popular all over the world. It is recognized as Canada's official gold investment coin and is the first bullion coin to be produced with 99.99% (24 carat) purity. To this day gold Maple Leaf coins are still produced with the same high purity and recognisable design. The coin is also considered legal tender throughout Canada. Maple Leaf gold coins are issued in a standard size of 1 Troy Ounce.
As well as this, the coin is also produced in smaller sizes of 1/2, 1/4, 1/10 and 1/20 Troy ounce editions. Its design features the maple leaf after which the coin is named after as well as being recognized as Canada's national emblem. The obverse of the coin holds the official portrait of Queen Elizabeth II and includes details of the coin's nominal value (50 dollars) and the year of issue. Maple Leaf coins are well-known throughout the world and therefore traded all around the world.

Australian Kangaroo

The 1 Oz Kangaroo 2022 is a coin minted in gold with a 999.9 purity and a spectacular Australian design. This coin series has remained a popular among both gold investors as well as coin collectors since it launched in 1986. Originally, this coin was called the Australian Nugget because its design featured the largest gold nugget ever found in Australia.
However, after 1989, this changed to a design of a kangaroo. This design is updated annually and is therefore also popular gold coin for collectors. For the year 2022 we see a detailed illustration a kangaroo with its joey in the Australian wilderness. The other side of Australian Kangaroo coin remains unchanged and features the portrait of Queen Elizabeth II.

American Eagle

The American Eagle is known as the first gold American coin to be produced for trading purposes. The coin came to market in 1986 by the United States Mint. The gold American Eagle is minted in a 916.7 purity, which is also known as 22 carat gold. This coin is minted exclusively from gold derived from American soil as stated in the 1985 Gold Bullion Act. The coin is adorned with American symbolism: the obverse shows “Walking Liberty,” goddess of freedom. In her right hand she holds a flaming torch. In her left she clutches an olive branch. The flipside is reserved for the eagle that has given its name to this gold coin
